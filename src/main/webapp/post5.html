Tsukuba University Opens in Malaysia: A New Chapter in Japan-Malaysia Educational and Cultural Ties

Published on: November 21, 2024

When I visited Tokyo in 2018, I mooted the idea of having Japan open its first university branch in Malaysia. Finally, today, I attended the opening of Tsukuba University, here in Malaysia, the first Japanese University to open outside of Japan. I am glad that this has come to fruition. This milestone marks a significant moment in the strengthening of educational and cultural ties between Japan and Southeast Asia.

When this idea was discussed with the late Shinzo Abe, the Japanese Prime Minister then, it was to enable Malaysian and southeast Asian students access to Japanese education at a lower cost. Apart from acquiring knowledge, the establishment of a Japanese university is intended to impart Japanese cultural values and the Japanese way of doing things, the discipline, hard work, dedication and etc. These values are vital in shaping individuals and communities, and I firmly believe they play a pivotal role in the success of any nation.

The culture of a people will determine whether they succeed or fail. I strongly believe in this when I went to Japan for first time in 1961. During that time, the country was in the midst of rebuilding after the devastation of World War II. Despite the challenges they faced, the Japanese were preparing for the 1964 Tokyo Olympics, a symbol of their recovery and ambition. What struck me the most is that they were working very hard even at that time to rebuild the country and eventually they became an economic power. I was very impressed by the Japanese discipline and culture of hard work. I believe that if we adopt some of these values, we too can achieve success as how the Japanese had succeeded.

It was this inspiration that led to the introduction of Malaysia’s “Look East Policy,” which sought to emulate the Japanese work ethic and discipline. Over the past 40 years, thousands of students have benefited from studying in Japan, and so too, Malaysia. Today, we are taking another step forward with the setting up of this Tsukuba University in Malaysia.The opening of Tsukuba University in Malaysia represents the next step in this journey, offering more students access to a world-class education grounded in the principles that have made Japan a global leader. The mutual benefits from this are immense and I would like to congratulate the first batch of 13 students, both from Malaysia and Japan.

This collaboration between Malaysia and Japan holds immense promise. The establishment of Tsukuba University not only facilitates academic exchange but also deepens the cultural connection between our two nations. Thank you very much and congratulations to the government of Japan for their support towards the setting up of Tsukuba University in Malaysia (UTMy). This is a proud moment for both our nations, and I am excited about the opportunities this partnership will create for future generations.
