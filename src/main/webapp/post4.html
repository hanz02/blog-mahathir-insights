Setting the Record Straight: My Stand Against Corruption Allegations

Published on: November 21, 2024

As a public servant who has dedicated decades of my life to the service of Malaysia, I feel compelled to address recent accusations against me regarding alleged corruption and mismanagement of wealth. These claims, though baseless, strike at the very core of my values and the principles I have upheld throughout my tenure in politics.

First and foremost, I wish to affirm that my income has always been derived from my official salary as a government servant. This income has been diligently accounted for and largely redirected towards causes aimed at uplifting the nation. Many of these resources were used to support political reforms, including efforts to address systemic corruption and remove those who betrayed the trust of the Malaysian people during the 1MDB scandal.

The allegations against me and my family, including the investigations into my two eldest sons, are part of a broader initiative purportedly aimed at eradicating corruption. While I fully support genuine anti-corruption efforts, I find it deeply disheartening that such initiatives are being weaponized for political purposes. These actions not only tarnish reputations but also detract from the real work of governance—addressing pressing issues like economic recovery, education, and social equity.

The relationship between myself and Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim has indeed been a long and complicated one, oscillating between alliance and opposition. However, it is crucial that our political differences not cloud the integrity of Malaysia’s institutions. I challenge those making these allegations to present concrete evidence to substantiate their claims. Transparency and accountability should apply to everyone, including those in positions of power today.

In my 22 years as Prime Minister and beyond, I have consistently championed the fight against corruption. I have also demonstrated that leadership requires sacrifice, often at great personal cost. Today, I am left with little wealth, a testament to my commitment to prioritizing the welfare of our people over personal gain.

These recent developments are not just an attack on my character; they are a test of the values we hold as a nation. Malaysia deserves leaders who prioritize unity over division, truth over deception, and progress over petty politics. My hope is that the focus of our political discourse shifts back to issues that truly matter to the rakyat.

As I continue to navigate these challenges, I remain steadfast in my commitment to integrity, transparency, and the pursuit of a better Malaysia. Let us remember that true leadership is measured not by wealth or power but by the ability to inspire trust, build consensus, and leave a lasting legacy of service.

Thank you for your continued trust and support. Together, we can ensure that Malaysia remains a beacon of hope and progress in a rapidly changing world.
